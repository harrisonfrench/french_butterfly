French Butterfly

The Eastern Black Swallowtail (Papilio polyxenes) is one of the most beautiful butterflies found in North America.

These stunning creatures are known for their striking black wings adorned with yellow spots and blue scaling. They can be found throughout eastern North America, from southern Canada to Florida and west to the Rocky Mountains.

Host Plants

Caterpillars feed on plants in the carrot family including parsley, dill, and fennel.

Wingspan

Adults have a wingspan of 3 to 4 inches, making them a medium-sized butterfly.

Flight Season

Active from April through November, with 2-3 generations per year.
